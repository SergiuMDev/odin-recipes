Spaghetti allo scoglio

Description

The Italian tradition of seafood cuisine boasts many important recipes such as spaghetti with clams or mussels and clams saut√® and regional delicacies such as brodetto fanese (fish soup) or fregola pasta with clams, just to name a few. But in many restaurants you probably happened to see or taste seafood spaghetti. Why not try them at home?

In this dish are all the scents of the Mediterranean that dress the spaghetti, partly cooked with the sauce to absorb the taste of the sea and become even more appetizing! There are countless versions of this delicacy. Here's ours, with clams, mussels and delicious scampi flesh! But feel free to add or replace the ingredients with razor clams, cuttlefish and baby octopus and enjoy seafood spaghetti that is always different and even more delicious!

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti - 320g
  • Clams - 1kg
  • Scampi - 8pcs
  • Extra vergin olive oil - 4 tbs
  • Parsley (to be chopped) - 1 sprig
  • Fine salt - to taste
  • Mussels - 1kg
  • Calamari (squid) - 300g
  • Cherry tomatoes - 300g
  • Garlic - 1 clove
  • White wine - 3 tbs
  • Black pepper - to pepper

Steps

  1. Start by cleaning the mussels and clams
  2. Place a colander over a container so that it does not touch the bottom and fill it with cold running water, leaving the clams to purge any sand
  3. Pour out the water and repeat, then leave them to soak
  4. Clean the mussels: remove any dirt on the shells with the back of a knife, if needed, and tear off the byssus, i.e. the "beard" that comes out of the shel
  5. Scrub clean with a new stainless steel pad
  6. It may happen that the shell of some clams contains only sand, to be sure that this is not the case, tap the shell on a chopping board decisively but not too hard, making sure that the opening is downwards. If you notice a black spot on the chopping board it means that it's full of sand and should therefore be discarded, but test also with a knife to see if it actually contains sand
  7. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large saucepan and when it is hot, pour in the clams and mussels
  8. Cover immediately with a lid and wait until they are all completely open, about 3-4 minutes
  9. Once the mussels and clams are open, remove the lid 7 and pour them into a container. Do not discard the cooking liquid but strain it through a tight mesh strainer and keep it warm
  10. Then shell both clams and mussels and set aside the flesh and some shells still full, while you can discard the empty shells
  11. Unless it's already cleaned, your calamari (squid) will need to be cleaned: remove the head, entrails and the outer skin and cut it open, then cut it into strips
  12. Then clean the scampi by cutting the sides of the tail with scissors and removing the shell that covers the abdomen. Using a toothpick, try to remove the gut, the black vein on the back of the scampi. This way it won't release a bitter taste.
  13. Now wash the tomatoes and cut them into wedges
  14. In the meantime, place a pot full of salted water on the stove and bring it to a boil, it will be used to cook the spaghetti
  15. Drizzle a pan with 2 tablespoons (25 g) of extra virgin olive oil and add a clove of garlic
  16. Once it is golden brown, pour in the squid and add a pinch of salt 17. Cook for 5 minutes then sweat with the white wine let the wine evaporate, remove the garlic, add the cherry tomatoes and cook for another 5 minutes. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti in boiling water
  17. Add the scampi to the sauce 22, season with salt and pepper
  18. Drain the spaghetti directly into the pan 4 minutes before their cooking time is up. Continue cooking and pour the mussels and clams cooking water you had set aside
  19. Lastly, add the shelled mussels and clams
  20. At the end of cooking, turn off the heat, season with a chopped sprig of parsley and toss one last time. Serve seafood spaghetti very hot
